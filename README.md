## 🔥 Energy Markets

### EN590 Diesel Trading
**EN590 diesel** is commercial diesel fuel meeting European standards and is one of the most traded products in global commerce.

**Key Information:**
- Standard: BS EN 590
- Trading Unit: Metric Ton (MT)
- Main Markets: ARA (Amsterdam), ULSD Futures
- Average Spread: 2-5 USD/MT
- Trading Volume: 200,000+ MT/day

[👉 EN590 Diesel Trading Guide](/wiki)

---

### Jet A1 Aviation Fuel
**Jet A1** is the standard jet fuel used in all commercial aviation. Compliant with ASTM D1655 and DEF STAN 91-91 specifications.

**Key Information:**
- Standard: ASTM D1655 / DEF STAN 91-91
- Trading Unit: Metric Ton (MT)
- Storage Temperature: -47°C
- Flash Point: 38°C minimum
- Market Size: 350+ million MT/year

[👉 Jet A1 Fuel Trading Guide](/wiki)

---

### LNG Trading
**Liquified Natural Gas (LNG)** is a critical energy source for electricity generation, heating, and industrial applications.

**Key Information:**
- Temperature: -162°C
- Transaction Volume: Commercial ship cargoes (135,000-180,000 m³)
- Price Indices: JKM, TTF, HH
- Geographic Arbitrage: High profit potential

[👉 LNG Trading Guide](/wiki)

---

### Bunker Fuel Trading
**Bunker fuels** represent 50-60% of ship operating costs and are central to maritime trade economics.

**Key Information:**
- Types: HFO (Heavy Fuel Oil), MDO (Marine Diesel Oil), MGO (Marine Gas Oil)
- Pricing: Spot and Forward markets
- Price References: Platts, Argus, Bunkerspot
- Trading Hubs: Singapore, Rotterdam, Fujairah

[👉 Bunker Fuel Trading Guide](/wiki)

---

### Crude Oil Trading
**Crude oil** is the most liquid commodity in the market. Brent, WTI, and Ural crudes are the main benchmarks.

**Key Information:**
- Brent Crude: Spot Laytime 15-23 days
- WTI: NYMEX futures
- Trading Unit: Barrels (BBL) and Metric Tons
- Global Trading: 90+ million BBL/day

[👉 Crude Oil Trading Guide](/wiki)

## 💼 Trade Finance

### What is CIS?
**CIS (Conformity to Contract Statement)** is the seller's formal declaration certifying that the product conforms to the order specifications.

**Contents:**
- Product Specifications
- Test Results (SGS, TÜV, etc.)
- Transportation and Insurance Details
- Seller Certification

[👉 CIS Guide](/wiki)

---

### How SBLC Works
**SBLC (Standby Letter of Credit)** is a financial instrument providing buyer protection in international trade transactions.

**Basic Mechanism:**
1. Buyer requests SBLC from exporter
2. Exporter obtains SBLC from their bank
3. Bank issues guarantee against credit limit
4. SBLC secures payment guarantee

[👉 SBLC Guide](/wiki)

---

### CIF vs FOB Explained
**CIF (Cost, Insurance, Freight)** and **FOB (Free on Board)** are the fundamental payment terms in international trade.

**Differences:**

| Term | Seller Responsibility | Buyer Responsibility | Risk Transfer |
|------|----------------------|----------------------|-----------------|
| **FOB** | Until ship loading | After ship loading | Ship ramp |
| **CIF** | Until destination port | After destination port | Destination port |

[👉 CIF vs FOB Guide](/wiki)

---

### What is ICPO?
**ICPO (Irrevocable Corporate Purchase Order)** is the buyer's irrevocable purchase commitment.

**Key Points:**
- Irrevocable and non-cancellable
- Legally binding
- Based on pro-forma invoice
- Specifies payment terms

[👉 ICPO Guide](/wiki)

---

### MT760 Explained
**MT760** is the SWIFT standard format through which banks send Letter of Credit (LC) notifications.

**Information:**
- Beneficiary (Recipient)
- Issuing Bank Confirmation
- Credit Limit
- Validity Dates

[👉 MT760 Guide](/wiki)

---

### What is a Soft Probe?
**Soft Probe** is a preliminary inquiry to assess buyer intentions and financial capacity before proceeding to formal quotations.

**Process:**
1. Collect buyer's basic information
2. Verify financial capacity
3. Assess payment ability
4. Conduct fraud checks

[👉 Soft Probe Procedure](/wiki)

---

### NCNDA & IMFPA Guide
**NCNDA (Non-Circumvention, Non-Disclosure Agreement)** and **IMFPA (International Mandate For Procurement Agreement)** form the legal framework of international trade.

**Protection:**
- Broker protection
- Confidentiality guarantees
- Trade secret protection

[👉 NCNDA & IMFPA Guide](/wiki)

## 📊 Common Trading Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Spot** | Price for immediate delivery |
| **Forward** | Agreement for future delivery date |
| **Spread** | Difference between bid and ask price |
| **Arbitrage** | Profiting from price differences across markets |
| **Leverage** | Using borrowed capital to increase position size |
| **Hedging** | Risk reduction through offsetting positions |
